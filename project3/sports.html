The RIT "Tigers" became the official mascot for the school in 1955-56, following an undefeated basketball season. Originally the mascot was called "Techmen" and the school colors were blue and gray. Harry Watts, former sports information director, saw the lack in team identification and suggested "Tiger" in 1955. About two years later, the school adopted burnt umber, orange, and white as the official school athletic colors.

Intramural Sports

There are intramural sports in everything from basketball to inner-tube water polo. There are tournaments that keep the competition up. There are more than 50% of students that participate in intramural sports each year, making it a great way to make friends. All participants must register on the Online Portal to play. Team captains must maintain accurate rosters on IMLeagues. If a player is not on a roster, they cannot participate. Also, players cannot participate in D1 and recreational for the same sport.

Leagues

  • Division 1: more competitve, players may have played the sport before or were varsity players or club team members.
  • Rec: not as competitve, players may be playing for the first time or don't have much experience.
  • Co-Ed: In order to form a co-ed team, there must be an enough women each week (Example: 3 women, 3 men, and a goalie in Soccer)
